Turnover Rate Analysis
Separate regrettable from non-regrettable turnover. The distinction drives retention strategy.
ℹTurnover rate = (Separations ÷ Average Headcount) × 100. Average headcount = (beginning + ending) ÷ 2. Voluntary regrettable turnover — exits you would have reversed — is the metric that should drive retention investment.
Employees whose exit you consider a loss to the mission.
Cost of Turnover Calculator
The true financial cost of a single departure. Most organizations dramatically underestimate this number — which is why retention investment never gets prioritized.
ℹSHRM and Center for American Progress research consistently shows that replacing a professional-level employee costs 50–200% of annual salary when all factors are included. Most organizations never see this number because the cost is distributed across budget lines with no one adding it up.
Exit Interview Analysis
Diagnostic analytics: identifying the root causes behind turnover. Categorize departures to surface patterns that aggregate data cannot show.
ℹVoluntary turnover rarely has one cause. Enter the number of exits attributed to each factor — a single departure may reflect multiple factors. Patterns in the top two or three categories point to specific organizational interventions, not generic retention programs.
Reasons Cited (can exceed total — multiple reasons per exit)
Engagement & Manager Effectiveness
Correlating engagement scores with manager span of control surfaces where people problems are actually management problems.
ℹResearch consistently shows that 70% of variance in team engagement is explained by the manager, not the organization. If engagement is low overall but high in certain teams, the intervention is management development — not a culture initiative. If it is uniformly low, the problem is structural.
Key Engagement Driver Scores (if available)
Attrition Risk Indicator
Predictive analytics: using organizational conditions to forecast which populations are at elevated risk of departure before they decide to leave.
△This tool scores structural attrition risk based on known predictors. It does not identify individuals — it identifies organizational conditions that statistically increase flight risk across a population. High scores indicate where retention investment should be concentrated before exits occur.
Labor Demand Forecast
Translate growth targets and operational plans into projected headcount requirements by role type and timeline.
Reduces net headcount need.
Succession & Talent Pipeline
Identify concentration risk and readiness gaps before they become emergency vacancies.
ℹSuccession planning is a predictive activity. The question is not who replaces someone — it is whether the organization can absorb the departure without a mission impact. Rate each critical role below for vacancy risk and internal readiness.
Priority Action Engine
Prescriptive analytics: synthesizing your data across all tabs to surface the highest-impact interventions in priority order.
◆This engine reads data entered across all other tabs and scores organizational issues by severity and mission impact. Complete the Workforce Snapshot and at least one other tab before generating recommendations. The more data entered, the more specific the output.
Enter workforce data across the other tabs, then click Generate Recommendations to surface priority actions.
Retention Investment ROI Model
Model the return on targeted retention investments against the cost of continued turnover.
Compensation adjustment, benefits upgrade, development budget, etc.
Compensation Budget Optimizer
Model merit budget allocation scenarios to maximize retention impact — who gets what, and why.
ℹUniform merit increases deliver the lowest retention return per dollar. Targeted allocation to employees below market midpoint or in high-risk segments returns 3–5x more retention value than across-the-board increases of the same total cost.
Pay Equity Indicators
High-level screening using aggregated averages. Use this to determine whether a formal equity analysis is warranted — not as a substitute for one.
ℹA formal pay equity analysis requires individual-level data, statistical controls for tenure, level, and function, and legal review. This tool provides an aggregate indicator only. If gaps appear here, engage employment counsel or a compensation specialist before communicating findings or taking corrective action.
