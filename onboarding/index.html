30 / 60 / 90 Day Goals
Set clear expectations for each phase. Share with new hire and supervisor before Day 1.
ℹ30-day goals focus on orientation and learning. 60-day goals introduce independent contribution. 90-day goals establish full operational effectiveness. Goals should be specific, measurable, and agreed upon in advance — not evaluated retroactively.
Day 1 Orientation Agenda
A structured first day is not logistics — it is a statement about organizational competence and care. Generate a customized agenda and share it with the new hire, their supervisor, and their onboarding buddy before the start date.
New Hire Announcement
Generate a team announcement that introduces the new hire with context — not just a name and title. People join organizations, but they stay for the people they work with.
Role-Specific Onboarding Path
ℹOnboarding an individual contributor is fundamentally different from onboarding a manager or an executive. The learning priorities, the relationship map, and the timeline for independent contribution all differ. Use the path below as a starting framework and adapt to the specific role.
Individual Contributor
Program staff, analysts, coordinators
Days 1–30
Learn the systems, meet the stakeholders, understand the context. Shadow existing team members on key workflows. First 30 days should have zero deliverables that matter.
Days 31–60
Begin independent contribution on lower-stakes tasks. Supervisor reviews all work and provides detailed feedback. Building technical competence and relationship credibility simultaneously.
Days 61–90
Producing work at the standard expected of the role. Trusted to manage their own workflows. Feedback moves from corrective to developmental.
Manager
Team leads, program managers, directors
Days 1–30
Listen before leading. Meet every direct report 1:1 within the first two weeks. Ask more than advising. Do not reorganize, restructure, or introduce new expectations until context is established.
Days 31–60
Diagnose the team: What is working? What is not? What are the dynamics? Where are the skill gaps? Begin setting clear expectations. Share preliminary observations with supervisor.
Days 61–90
Present a team assessment and 6-month plan to supervisor. Begin active performance management. Cross-functional relationships established.
Executive / Director+
VPs, C-suite, Executive Directors
Days 1–30
Stakeholder mapping and relationship investment. Meet board members, key funders, major partners, and all direct reports. Listen to the organization's stories about itself — the narrative always contains the diagnosis.
Days 31–60
Internal assessment: financial health, operational gaps, team strengths and liabilities, strategic coherence, cultural patterns. Begin identifying the 2–3 priorities that will define the first year.
Days 61–90
Present a 90-day findings document to the board. Introduce a 12-month strategic framework. Demonstrate coherent leadership voice. Be visible — internally and externally.
System Access & Equipment Provisioning
Track every account, credential, and piece of equipment that needs to be ready before Day 1.
△System access provisioning is the most common onboarding failure point. A new hire who cannot access email or tools on Day 1 signals organizational dysfunction — not just inconvenience. Complete this checklist at least 5 business days before the start date. For executive hires, allow 10 business days.
New Hire Integration Guide
A complete, print-ready onboarding document — cover page, Day 1 agenda, 30/60/90 plan, system access checklist, and team announcement. Distribute to the new hire, supervisor, and onboarding buddy before the start date.
ℹThis guide compiles everything entered across all tabs into a single, formatted document. Complete the New Hire Setup and Goals tabs before generating. The guide is designed to be printed and handed to three people: the new hire, their supervisor, and their onboarding buddy.
Included in Guide
✓ Cover page with org and hire details
✓ Pre-start preparation checklist
✓ Day 1 orientation agenda
✓ 30 / 60 / 90 day plan with success criteria
✓ System access provisioning status
✓ Team announcement draft
Recommended Distribution
New hire: Full guide
Supervisor: Full guide
Buddy: Day 1 agenda + system access
IT / Ops: System access page only
File: Retain full guide in personnel file
Timing
Generate and distribute at least 3 business days before the start date. Send the new hire their copy with a personal note from the supervisor. Do not send a guide without a human touch attached to it.
